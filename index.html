Problem Statement

Credit unions are drowning in data they can't use

Credit unions and community banks struggle to convert their disparate, raw data — from core systems, Eltropy's interaction channels, CRM, and more — into meaningful, timely, and actionable intelligence.

"We have all this data sitting in five different systems and no one can tell me what's actually happening with our members right now."

— VP of Marketing, mid-size credit union

🔒

Lack of Accessible Tools & Expertise

Credit unions lack a self-service BI tool easy enough for non-analysts (executives, managers, marketing VPs). They rely on manual, slow reporting or complex systems requiring dedicated data analysts.

🗂️

Data Fragmentation

Critical data is siloed across multiple systems — member communications in Eltropy, account data in core banking. This prevents unified, cross-source analysis needed to understand key business drivers.

⏳

Inefficiency in Insight Generation

Executives and managers spend excessive time compiling reports and digging for insights rather than receiving proactive, clear, and contextualised summaries of what has changed and why.

Design Approach

Four pillars that fill a critical gap for community banks

The design goal: turn disparate data into meaningful, timely insights using the power of generative AI — without requiring any technical expertise from the user.

📋

Predefined Dashboards — Interaction Insights

Ready-made analytics covering each Eltropy solution area — eliminating the need for users to build reports from scratch.

Digital Channels Engagement: Unified overview of Text Messaging, Secure Chat, Remote Video, and Co-Browse.
Appointment Management: Tracks bookings, completions, and no-shows.
Lobby Management: Branch traffic, wait times, and service times.
🔧

Custom Dashboards

Users can create dashboards from scratch or modify pre-built ones to fit their team's specific needs.

Create from scratch: Name, description, and AI auto-populates initial widgets.
Cross-filtering: Click a chart element (e.g. a branch bar) to filter all other charts.
Standard filters: Date range, branch, agent — available on every dashboard.
💬

Natural Language Search — "Ask Intelligence"

Type questions in plain English, receive instant visual answers. As intuitive as Googling something.

Powered by ThoughtSpot's NLQ engine for high-accuracy query parsing.
Conversational BI: Follow-up queries refine results without losing context (e.g. "break that down by member age").
Suggested queries and business glossary terms surface in the search bar to guide analysis.
✦

AI-Generated Insights & Alerts

Proactively surfacing what matters — so users never have to dig for answers themselves.

AI Highlights: Auto-surfaces notable changes and trends when a dashboard is opened.
Natural Language Narratives: Plain-language explanations of charts help non-technical users interpret findings.
Threshold Alerts: e.g. if video sessions fall below 50 — notify via email + in-app with AI explanation.
Anomaly Detection: Automatic alerts when a metric deviates significantly from the norm.
Discovery Script & Outcomes

What we asked, what we learned

Our discovery sessions used a before/after protocol — testing assumptions before showing the solution, then validating decisions after.

Before showing the solution

When you come to analytics/intelligence, what data would be most helpful to show upfront?

On the dashboard listing page, which information is most important to see at a glance in the overview cards?

Do KPIs in the overview cards help you make decisions, or would an AI-generated summary be more useful?

After showing the solution

Which section do your eyes go to first — and why?

Do the insights shown here give you enough context to take action immediately?

Do the trend indicators (+/- %) help you decide if something needs attention? What's missing?

What would you do if this metric dropped? What's missing here for you to take action?

Key design decisions unlocked by research

Two standout findings directly changed the product direction.

⭐

Watchlist instead of Favourites

Users pushed back on the "Favourites" model. What they actually wanted was a Watchlist — the ability to flag and monitor specific metrics, not entire dashboards. This reframe changed the entire navigation and notification architecture.

🎛️

Customisable KPIs on dashboard card views

The initial design showed fixed KPIs in each dashboard's card/overview. Users unanimously needed the ability to choose which KPIs appear in their card view — different roles care about completely different metrics. This led to a per-user KPI personalisation feature.
